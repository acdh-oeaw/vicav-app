Mission
The Vienna Corpus of Arabic Varieties (VICAV) is an international project aiming at the collection of digital language resources documenting varieties of spoken Arabic. It provides a wide range of materials such as language profiles, dictionaries, annotated texts, bibliographies and more.
The Vienna Corpus of Arabic Varieties has been started as a co-operation between the Department of Oriental Studies of the University of Vienna and the Austrian Centre for Digital Humanities (ACDH) of the Austrian Academy of Sciences.
Project Outline
The Vienna Corpus of Arabic Varieties (VICAV) is an international project aimed at the collection of digital language resources documenting varieties of spoken Arabic. It is meant to provide a variety of relevant materials such as language profiles (concise linguistic descriptions of particular varieties), dictionaries, annotated texts, bibliographies and more.
VICAV has been started as a co-operation between the Department of Oriental Studies of the University of Vienna and the Austrian Centre for Digital Humanities (ACDH) of the Austrian Academy of Sciences. It is also part of CLARIAH.AT’s project bundle striving to make available more language resources for under-resourced linguistic varieties.
Apart from its linguistic goals, the project has been pursuing strong interests in various aspects of text technology. The output of the work of the contributors is published in form of different types of digital language resources. The ACDH’s research interests in this project have been related to issues of digital lexicography, visualisation of digital language resources in a multilingual environment und the application of standards and (de-facto) standards such as TEI and/or LMF in the creation of digital language resources.
Contributors
The project has been conducted as a co-operation between the Department of Oriental Studies of the University of Vienna and the Institute of Corpus Linguistics and Text Technology of the Austrian Academy of Sciences. It is also part of CLARIN-AT’s project bundle aiming at more language resources for under-resourced linguistic varieties.
VICAV is a collaborative undertaking. We are aiming at serving the community at large and want to be as inclusive as possible. Contact us if you want to participate in our efforts.

Linguistics
The VICAV Research Environment has been designed as a tool for the presentation of digital material concerning Arabic dialectology. It was intended as a means to promote the efficient exchange of ideas and experiences in an active international community.
VICAV serves as an interface between areal and corpus linguistics. The aim is to gather varying digital language resources for a number of different localities. The description of the different varieties hinges on a particular type of text which we call ’language profiles’, concise and standardised ‘form sheets’ that display uniformly arranged information on research history, available literature, salient grammatical features etc. The website will also make accessible bibliographies, dictionaries, glossaries, and different types of transcribed texts.
The project proceeds from two basic assumptions: In Arabic dialectology, information on new findings and current developments is mainly exchanged at international conferences and in scientific journals. Due to a steadily increasing number of projects which produce digital data too there is an increasing need to make these data quickly available to others in the field. There already exist a couple of individual webpages which - due to the lack of resources - often do not match professional standards. There is a big gap between the linguistics communities and Arabic Studies in general and Arabic dialectology in particular. Linguistic studies in Arabic dialects have widely ignored the works of traditional, i.e. philologically based, dialectology and vice versa. We are convinced that VICAV has the potential to help to bridge this gap to the benefit of both disciplines.
